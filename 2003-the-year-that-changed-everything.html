2003 - the year that changed everything

Chamar boy | 26 Sep 2020 | Updated on 18 Apr 2021

26 Sept 2003

I think i passed engineering. Not happy but relieved.

I am oscillating between a deep sense of regret of not doing the things I wanted to do in engineering and the relief that I survived despite all odds.

As part of the first batch of engineering from this college, there are not many prospects to start with. Though I am trying for jobs, my heart knows that i will not get any - grades and the way I looked - deformed bald and itchy.

I am trying to prepare for GATE, to finally do the nerdy stuff which i am very capable of doing. 4 years of engineering taught me a valuable lesson - people are highly ordinary - not capable of creating anything new, just masters of mugging and following the orders.

The legal storm caused by my sisters adventures finally seems to be over, my father seems to have it handled. I never disclosed it to anyone, but I am prepared to do anything if the situation goes worse. Every night I evaluate the strategy, the tools and the things I need to be careful about. This decision is a combined result of my current state, my duties as a brother and my complete belief that life would not be any different from what I have experienced it till now. Life in prison sounded way real and acceptable than spending years with the scared yet competitive sheep of engineering college graduates.

My mom and father love each other. I cant make sense of it. My father is a principled, hard working individual, though my mother is really nothing but manipulative, power hungry, ugly excuse of a female. It is not like only I hated her, all of us did, except my father.

At least once a month, my mother and father would take off to a date of sorts - they would leave in morning and come back late at night. This has been one of the happiest days of the month for me and my sisters for years.

Off late, I have distanced myself from my second sister. I like being alone and destructive, a man who has no one and nothing to loose. The people around me are too busy to cater to their plans to take over the world, who would care about a discolored, bottom of the class chamar boy? Hey, my answer is the same as you, hence the going out in brotherly glory plan.

I am happy at the prospect of never going back to the college again, forced to communicate with those judgmental pricks of classmates, teachers who would congratulate me and suggest that i would get a fat sarkari job now. Somehow Infy, TCS, Polaris etc are never discussed with me.

Mom and Dad left for their monthly date, sister is out doing whatever she does. I am up, go to the computer room and dial up the Internet. Checking mail, yeah....no prospects, the self destruct plan stands. Phone rings.

I hate phone....she never calls, and she wont. She knows now, at-least suspects it...me being a chamaar. How can the shiniest, most popular girl date a chamar? Anyways, I will explain the situation later.

I answer, the digits seem similar - the call is from our family cell phone. I pick up, though i do not recognize the voice. The voice is of my uncle, he is trying to tell me something important in a very non important way. The car has broken down, and i need to come to get it fixed. I should get a friend to drive my bike back so I could drive the car after getting it repaired. The location is near a hospital. I say yes, and did not argue how illogical the plan sounded. In the background i could hear a lot of commotion. Anyways, i needed to patch up my appearance and drive a bike with no rear brakes for 100 kms. When you are on a death wish, these choices are really not a choice.

I call up a friend, a fellow SC/ST boy who flunked and now is a junior. Caste seems to be the only quality which defines my life right now. I am afraid of the general friends - ex girl friend might have blabbered..the news might have spread....and i have lost my looks. Most of people avoid me in college, the friends i thought i had left me because a girl who seems to have loved me, told everyone that i molested her. I never did. I literally and figuratively had lost my face and most friends with it.

SC friend shows up. He is riding pillion and i am driving my Bajaj caliber chroma. My friend is asking a lot of questions, i don't have any answers.

I drive, having some non-sense chatter with friend. My mind is racing though. Maybe my grandmother is sick or died and maybe my dad is too broken up to drive. This seemed to be the most logical interpretations of the facts i could recall.

After an hours drive, we arrive at a hospital. As i enter, I could see 100s of faces I had forgotten. They seemed to be all watching me closely. I am not sure if its due to my current appearance or due to the incident.

Something is amiss. The damn car seems fine. People are crying, I am trying to find someone talk-able. Someone directs me to a sub group. Mother is crying, trying to be sad than she is.

Father is dead.

How, why and what the fuck? Did her stupidity killed him or was it an assault? I am enraged, hoping it was an assault....and i get to kill someone for it...finally justify my existence. It was a heart attack.

I don't understand...are they sure? This is a good private hospital...they must be sure. I could have reacted way more naturally had I was not surrounded by literally 100s of people. Indians love drama, and i was customary bound to provide it today.

Something has hardened me enough to disobey conventions, and to realize what it means.....I am no longer the kid brother, or someone who can ask for help. With my stupid mom and uncontrollable sister.....what the literal fuck?? I need help to come to terms of what is happening to me and now this added responsibility too?

Nothing is fair. As my brain is processing the implications, I am interrupted - there are choices to be made. Where to do the kriyas? I don't know. I am thinking here. I have my uncles, and I do not know what is to be done. My mother interferes. She turns off her crying for a moment and tells me to tell them Rohini, at our new home. I want to protest, but then a dollop of emotional superiority infused cold logic shrewd logic is disclosed....we need to keep up the reputation. I just want to be alone, as soon as possible. I say yes. Handing over the keys of the bike to the friend i tell him to be careful, the rear brakes are not working, the front ones do, but they are more effective than one anticipates.

Life would never be the same again.
