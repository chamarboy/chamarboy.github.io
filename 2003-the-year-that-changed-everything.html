2003 - the year that changed everything

Chamar boy | 26 Sep 2020 | Updated on 27 Jan 2021

26 Sept 2003

I think i passed engineering. Not happy but relieved. I am oscillating between a deep sense of regret of not doing the things I wanted to do in engineering and the relief that I survived despite all odds.

As part of the first batch of engineering from this college, there are not many prospects to start with. Though I am trying for jobs, my heart knows that i will not get any - grades and the way I looked - deformed bald and itchy.

I am trying to prepare for GATE, to finally do the nerdy stuff which i am very capable of doing. 4 years of engineering taught me a valuable lesson - people are highly ordinary - not capable of creating anything new, just masters of mugging and following the orders.

The legal storm caused by my sisters adventures finally seems to be over, my father seems to have it handled. I never disclosed it to anyone, but I am prepared to do anything had the situation gone any bad. Every night I evaluate the strategy, the tools and the things I need to be careful about. This decision is a combined result of my current state, my duties as a brother and my complete belief that life would not be any different from what I had experienced in college. Life in prison sounded way real and acceptable than spending years with the scared yet competitive sheep of engineering college graduates.

My mom and father love each other. I cant make sense of it. My father is a principled hard working individual, though my mother is really nothing but manipulative, power hungry, ugly excuse of a female. It is not like only I hated her, all of us did, except my father.

At least once a month, my mother and father would take off to a date of sorts - they would leave in morning and come back late at night. This has been one of the happiest days of the month for me and my sisters for years.

Off late, I have distanced myself from my second sister. I like being alone and destructive, a man who has no one and nothing to loose. The people around me are too busy to cater to their plans to take over the world, who would care about a discolored, bottom of the class chamar boy? Hey, my answer is the same as you, hence the going out in brotherly glory plan.

I am happy at the prospect of never going back to the college again, forced to communicate with those judgmental pricks of classmates, teachers who would congratulate me and suggest that i would get a fat sarkari job now. Somehow Infy and TCS etc were never discussed with me.
