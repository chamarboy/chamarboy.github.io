Introduction

Chamar boy | 01 Sep 2020 | Updated on 30 Mar 2021

Who

I am a Chamar boy, born in a somewhat normal middle class (not Chamar middle class, normal middle class) family.

I was born in 1981 in a semi-urban village in Delhi.

My father had a Bajaj Super in 1990, and we got a landline telephone in 1997.

I know it's hard to imagine a village in Delhi now, but in 1990 till the time we stayed there, it had a water well, we had cows and cow dung was the primary fuel though my father got us LPG ever since i can remember.

So now here we are. Year is 2020, the most chaotic year for my generation, but somehow I am better placed than I ever was.

I have a spacious (in most senses) house, 2 cars, 2 dogs, a beautiful and intelligent son and mostly loving wife.

And I work at Facebook.

That is the big reveal. 2 Cr.

That declaration is not an achievement, I just want my readers to know what they are dealing with here.

This Facebook and the money thing seems important to society.

Apparently it kind of justifies my existence. People are nicer.

Hypocrisy of society becomes very apparent when you are even minutely successful.

Ever since I have realized what Chamar is, and how apparently we have special powers given by the constitution, a subject of envy and inflammatory debates on social media and hushed discussion in social circles.

Why

As I am getting older, I do tend to think about the past, mostly to learn from it. I remember those days, when my self worth was negative million.

I was targeted, gaslit at college, did not take it well, slipped in a depression, developed Psoriasis, spent years in a sham ayurvedic ashram with skin swollen like a balloon filled with pus covered in cow dung.

Abandonment by the girl I loved, the girl who loved me, friends, my own family. A failed suicide attempt, living through vengeance, death of father, a sister which destroyed the family, destroyed my existence.

All because I am a Chamar.

It was not all bad, It somehow made me realize the strengths which I thought I never had, and did generally well. Something kept me pushing, and I kept breathing.

I want to share my journey with you. Anyone who feels like I did in my Chamar years can get some semblance of hope or direction and hopefully he/she does not commit the grave mistakes I did, or felt like how I felt.

To Readers

I hope you get a balanced view of a Chamar life and hopefully next time you feel like feeling taller, maybe you will be empathetic to the SC/ST humanoid in your class, office or school.

If you feel like saying stuff, you have the full freedom to give me a piece of your mind, absolutely brutally.

I have will update the blog in a way that anonymous are welcome and give me their best shot. It's sort of a fuel to me now.

To my fellow Chamaar/SC/ST Readers

I hope you learn.

I hope you feel hopeful.

I hope you can believe in yourself, rather than what society is telling you silently.

If my journey can benefit any one of you, my efforts would make sense to me.

How

I will try to narrate it in a novel kind of style.

You already know where I am.

Logically my story starts in class 11, when I started realizing the implications of being a chamar, what it meant for career, for making friends, girls and neighborhood.

I have changed certain key details, to preserve the identity of the people involved.

I will narrate the journey, leaving the judgment to readers.
