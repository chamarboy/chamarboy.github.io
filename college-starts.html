College Starts

Chamar boy | 01 Sep 1999 | Updated on 18 Apr 2021
  • No more Maths!!
  • I am in love with my dream girl Gunjan, and she seems to love me too
  • Got into a prestigious engineering college, that too on a free seat
  • Mom and Dad seem to be proud.
  • I have loyal friends who are fun and inspiring too
  • Sisters are happy in their own worlds

After six months of anxiety, Everything seems to be going my way. World is a fair place, life is fair.

Then why is my spidey sense tingling ? I should be happy, content and optimistic.....but the tingling is growing bigger, unavoidable , something that demands urgency.

As I am thinking, the pessimistic chamar boy inside me gets another opportunity to tell me to recall the lessons imparted by my father. The mini horror stories which my father seems to hide, or sometimes he would try to share a varnished version of his rough experiences being chamar boy senior.

His stories were often re-runs, though his narration and emotions would change each time he recounted his experiences.

I tried to listen, though my sisters seemed to phase them out. I think being older they have listened to these stories more than me, and now they know the story but cant seem to relate the message to current circumstances.

A father walks a difficult path, whether to fill his kids with optimism, or to warn them about how fucked up the real world is. How men turn into farm animals – just give them privacy and some leverage over the other person.

This is what i have gotten to know so far from my father and his choice of composition of his re run stories.

For last 2 years, things have been bitter in the house. Ever since my elder sister ran off to marry a brahmin boy, my father seems to have taken a rather pessimistic view of life. His sentences indicate dejection, sound impersonal and formal. It seemed like his faith in his kids has been lost. Every tone sounds defensive, as if he is trying to distance us from the decisions we may take. He became a very formal father. There may have an attempt to acknowledge me as an adult member of the house, which translated to more duties and expectations without any chance of failure.

There may have been some resentment from my side too....considering i have been physically and publicly beaten by my father on a few occasions. Last incident was just few months back.

I will finally get a computer!!! That is the gain i have focused my attention on. I have earned it.

Gunjan seems happy, we talk everyday at night...the telephone bills have been noticed, so i call her from a public phone-booth owner by a creepy guy. Rs 1 for 3 minutes of awkward silences, random talk. I do get probed for lost time and begging for more pocket-money is a weekly ritual....just to hear her voice.

Gunjan and I share a something spiritual... a level of emotional intimacy that is hard to describe. I know she is damaged, misunderstood, happy, having a very desirable female form which attracts all kinds of attention.

I have not told her about my feelings for her, and she makes extra efforts to make me feel like another extra in her life....she likes me, waits for my call, gives me attention, tries to keep tabs on me too...but the beauty of it all has been ....i always felt insecure...her words express indifference though her tone is always enticing....a riddle that i can never solve.

Elder sister visits, though her visits are very stressful.

Younger sister is not doing well though. I never identified with her...her values, her approach to problem analysis and solutions has been highly circumspect. Ever since my elder sister eloped, we look out for each other, though I do not understand her choices. She is my new support system. She is struggling with studies, personal relationships though she seems to be happy and optimistic.

My mother has become more viscous. She seems to be always on lookout for anything remotely suspicious. Our college timings, friends....everything was her area of attention and instant judgment.

Most nights, my father and mother would drink up senseless. Me and my sister were assigned the ground floor, mother and father used to occupy first floor. At ten, we would depart to our respective rooms. In my estimate, my father and mother consume 3-4 pegs of whiskey along with a beer each before losing the sense of loudness and awareness.

I could hear her inciting my father everyday on either of these few issues:

  • how their own daughter betrayed them
  • how their siblings have betrayed them
  • how my sister and i are following in our elder sisters footsteps.

Some nights, my mother would cover a few issues and on really bad ones, they would cover all, get drunker than usual and call despicable names for all .... including us.

My nights were simple - trying to call Gunjan at nights, be on guard if things go bad.

The really bad nights were the one where my mother would succeed in finding something which was remotely true....and my sister’s struggle in her life would give her periodic ammunition. Special night horror show used to start with our parents discussing about my sister or me, in an extremely loud, obnoxiously oblivious way...as if they deliberately want us to hear their messaging, giving them benefit of doubt of being drunk. After 25-40 minutes of this intentional yet unintentional drama, my father would call out for both of us. These edge of seat, awaiting the dreaded call-out moments kept me anxious to say the least. I have to keep listening, to be ready to defend us, if and when we would be called out to discuss issues in my chamaar family style.

My father has 2 very noticeable flaws

  1. He is and has been afraid of conflict.
  2. His judgment seems to be influence-able, proven by the fact that my illogical, viscous mom had succeeded in isolating him from the world he nurtured – our village where we were genuinely cared for. His own kids seem like a liability to him now

My uncles -father’s brothers have been the best tutors to nurture us as kids, we being the initial ones in our unusually big joint family. Our extended family was respected and feared at the same time. My granddad had a larger than life image – his tool was his aggression, flamboyance and the original devil may care attitude.

My father and uncles were an exact mirror opposite image of my grandfather. Logical, careful, painfully aware of their status in society’s pecking order. All of them made out of the same material as my grandmother – a genuine diplomat with extremely caring personality – perfectly complementary characteristics to my grandfather. My time spend in this environment has been the most happiest, blissful period of my life. This deserves a separate post later.

Coming back to spidey sense....it has been tingling ever since my elder sister has eloped, every night has been a torture, a reminder of how i need to run away from home as soon as i start earning. I have a whole plan – i would move to a different city, elope with Gunjan and live happily ever after and telling about my truth in the deathbed. She is everything i need!! and sometimes she helps me distance from my fucked up nights. Though I cannot afford love in current circumstances, I need her…she is the only normal thing in my teenage life, my only sense of intimacy of thought...a sense of belonging, loving.

It is 10, and horror show resumes : S03E234.

I have the luxury of space – I have taken the living room at ground floor as my room. The room is directly above our functional living room, where my mom and dad discuss their daily challenges and defeats. I can hear when they move their glasses or go to the balcony. I try to not be in this room when these drunken conversations are happening. Cannot call Gunjan right now, I might be called in anytime upstairs, then I would have to explain her about the abrupt phone hangup. Also her phone is rarely available from 10-11, I prefer to call her after my parents conclude their night party.

ChamarBoy, come upstairs….we need to discuss something.

The dreaded call has come. Why now, why today...my mind is racing. My sister is also called. I get out...i can see my sister standing in the hallway too. This time the issue seems to be about her academics...though my father thinks of these meetings as a bonus to teach me.

We try to get out of it, by making feeble excuses, but I am not sure if those were heard. We have become somewhat used to this routine. We are smiling outside with our minds racing.

The stairs are dark, sister (lets call her rani delusional) lits up the dreaded U shape stairway. We start walking. As we reach in the middle to take the U step, mom unbolts the 1st floor entry gate.

“Sab sach batana warna ghar se nikal denge tere papa….tumhe humari life mein problem create karne ke alawa aata hi kya hai……

..

…”

Mom’s background abuses are faded away as we enter the living room. The smell and the moment remind of your place in this house.

Shaadi karni hai ya padhai?

I looked up – It is not a question you may ask your 17 year son. I am not sure if he asked me or my sister?

He was looking at me.

5 seconds pass, it cant be that obvious ? Can it be?

“padhai”

“Toh padhta kyun nahin hai?”

“papa mein padh raha hoon”

“Tera admission kitni pareshani ke baad hua hai, bhagte bhoot ki langoti pakdi hai tune, kabhi pura kaam bhi kar liya kar”

He has a point there – I cant seem to get anything completed. I do the bare minimum and take my chances. Too much life is happening around me. Gunjan takes away most of my time and attention.

Papa, I am doing well now (I really am not)

After a couple of more awkward questions, we are signaled to leave. It is 12 now, new day has started.

Is Gunjan up??

I hurry to my room and hookup a stolen/borrowed landline phone into the telephone sockets which I have secretly extended to my room. This is a lineman’s phone – big yellow with rotary dial. One of my friends had gifted this phone to me – I think he stole it when lineman was up in the pole and left his work bag on ground. What a genuine buddy!!

It is ringing………..no response…...is she angry?? Is she done with me?? Has she found someone better ( everyone is better!!)??….maybe she wants me to feel disposable….she is a weird girl...i think she sabotages things..when they become real…

No response. I decide not to try again….she might be sleeping or she is ignoring me...either ways I cant seem to sleep.

Things with Gunjan have been complicated to say the least. A loser general boy who couldn't not even clear BBA exams told her that I belong to SC/ST category, hence I am able to clear the entrance exams. This is not a false statement...it was my truth to tell….i wanted to tell her when I was ready.

It devoids me of the privilege of telling my truth personally, and I would have told her when I was sure of my worthiness. Yes I have been lying to her….self preservation reasons mainly. A prom queen cannot be seen with a chamar boy….that much was clear to me.

I could see her slipping away...she has been acting aloof...even by her hot girl persona.

Love or even creating a possibility of love is an expensive proposition for anyone at my standards. Even I ignore the substantial emotional baggage and guilt I have been carrying….my world was built on a false identity….all of which may collapse once my truth is out.

And there are college studies too...12 exams in a curtailed 1st semester in 4 months including my demon - Mathematics!
